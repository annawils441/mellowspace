Mindful Observation
Mindfulness is the act of doing one thing at a time whilst only focusing on it and it alone.
Observe the items around you, one by one.
Naming them as you observe.

Object Focus
Focus on one object, describe it in immense detail. What does it
look, smell and feel like?

Body Scan
1. Find a comfortable position, either sitting or lying down.
2. Close your eyes if you feel comfortable doing so.
3. Take a few slow, steady breaths.
4. Begin by focusing your attention on your feet. Notice any sensations, such as warmth, pressure, tingling, or relaxation.
5. Slowly move your attention upwards through your legs, hips, stomach, chest, shoulders, arms, hands, neck, and head.
6. Spend a few moments observing each area without judging or trying to change anything.
7. If your mind wanders, gently bring your focus back to the part of the body you were observing.
8. Once you've reached the top of your body, take a final deep breath and slowly return your attention to your surroundings.

Breathing Techniques

Box Breathing - Imagine you are
going around a box with your
breathing. Breathe in for 4, hold
for 4 and exhale for 4.

Paced Breathing - Breathe in for
4, hold for 7 and exhale for 8.

Triangle Breathing - Breathe in
for 3, hold for 3 and exhale for 3.

Counted Breaths
1. Breathe in and think "one."
2. Breathe out and think "two."
3. Continue counting up to ten.
4. If your mind wanders, start again at one.

Physical Wellbeing

Stretching can help release physical tension
that builds up throughout the day,
particularly during periods of stress or anxiety.
Taking just a few minutes to stretch can improve flexibility, encourage relaxation
and provide an opportunity to pause and
focus on the present moment.

Good quality sleep is essential for
maintaining mental and physical health.
Establishing a consistent sleep routine,
creating a relaxing bedtime environment,
and limiting distractions before bed can help improve sleep quality and support
emotional wellbeing.

Walking is a simple yet effective way to
support mental wellbeing. Whether it's a
short walk around your neighbourhood or a
longer walk in nature, regular movement can
help reduce stress, improve mood and
provide a break from overwhelming
thoughts or daily pressures.

Staying hydrated is an important part of
self-care. Even mild dehydration can affect
concentration, energy levels and mood.
Setting reminders to drink water throughout
the day can help you maintain healthy
hydration habits and support your overall
wellbeing.

Distractions

Write a pros and cons list for
an action urge. Pros/Cons of
acting on the urge then
Pros/Cons of not acting
on the urge.

Choose activities that encourage
emotions different from the ones
you're currently experiencing.
(Opposite Action) Listen to happy
music, look through happy photos,
watch funny cat videos on YouTube.

Temporarily set aside distressing
thoughts when you are unable to
address them immediately.
Visualise placing worries in a box.
Focus fully on a task/hobby.

Use safe physical sensations
to ground yourself in the
present moment such as ice,
a warm shower,
light a candle.

Engage in an activity that

Redirect your attention by

Tired

Rest without guilt - Give yourself
permission to rest. Taking breaks and
allowing your body and mind to
recover is an important part of
wellbeing.

Get natural light - Exposure to
daylight can help regulate your body's
internal clock and improve alertness.
Consider sitting near a window or
spending a few minutes outside.

Nourish your body - Eating regular
meals and snacks can help maintain
energy throughout the day. Try to
choose foods that provide sustained
energy rather than quick bursts
followed by crashes.

Reduce stimulation - Consider taking
a short break from notifications,
social media or other distractions
that may be contributing to
mental fatigue.

Hydrate - Even mild dehydration can
contribute to feelings of tiredness and difficulty concentrating.
Try drinking a glass of water and
keeping a bottle nearby
throughout the day.

Gentle movement - While
intense exercise may feel overwhelming,
light stretching or a short walk
can sometimes help boost energy levels
and reduce feelings of sluggishness.

One small task - When you're
exhausted, focus on completing one
simple task rather than tackling
a long to-do list.
Small achievements can help reduce
feelings of overwhelm.

Sleep hygiene check - Review your
sleep routine. Consistent bedtimes,
reduced screen time before bed
and a comfortable sleep environment
can support better quality rest.

Distress Tolerance

STOP Skill -
S - Stop what you're doing.
T - Take a step back.
O - Observe your thoughts, feelings
and surroundings.
P - Proceed mindfully.

Self Soothing -
- Listen to calming music.
- Light a scented candle.
- Wrap yourself in a soft blanket.
- Enjoy a warm drink.

Paced Breathing -
Inhale for 4 seconds.
Exhale for 6 seconds.
Continue for several minutes.

Pros & Cons -
Before acting on anger, consider:
- What are the benefits of reacting immediately?
- What are the risks?
- What are the benefits of waiting?
- How might I feel about this tomorrow?

Leave the situation -
take time-out from it to
allow yourself to regulate.

TIPP Skill -
T - Temperature change
I - Intense exercise
P - Paced breathing
P - Paired muscle relaxation

Understanding Anger

Anger Journal -
Write down:
- What happened?
- What thoughts went through your mind?
- How intense was your anger (1-10)?
- What did you need in that moment?
This can help identify triggers and patterns over time.

Identify the real emotion - Anger is sometimes
a "secondary emotion."
Ask yourself:
- Am I actually hurt?
- Am I feeling rejected?
- Am I embarrassed?
- Am I feeling anxious?
Understanding the underlying emotion can make it easier to
address the problem.

Thought Reframing -
Instead of:
"They never listen to me."
Try:
"I'm frustrated because I don't feel heard
right now."
